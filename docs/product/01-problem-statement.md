# Problem Statement

## Background

Self-directed learners increasingly rely on long-form lecture videos as a primary learning source. These materials are useful for depth, but they are difficult to navigate once the learner needs to revisit a specific concept later.

## Current Pain

After watching a lecture, the learner may remember that an explanation, example, or definition appeared somewhere in the media, but not where. The memory is often strong enough to know the content exists, but too vague to recover the exact segment quickly.

## Why The Current Manual Workflow Is Insufficient

The common fallback is to rewatch, scrub through the timeline, or scan a full transcript if one exists. This is slow, interruptive, and unreliable, especially for long videos. The effort required to recover one small piece of knowledge is often disproportionate to the value of the answer.

## Why This Problem Is Worth Solving

This is a repeated, practical problem in real learning workflows. A focused system that helps users search and jump to the right segment can reduce friction, improve study efficiency, and make previously consumed learning media more reusable. The problem is narrow enough for an MVP while still being meaningful as a real product.
